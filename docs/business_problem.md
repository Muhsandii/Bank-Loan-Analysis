# Business Problem

## Background

A lending company provides personal loans to customers across different states, purposes, income levels, and credit grades.

Managing a loan portfolio requires balancing profitability with credit risk. Poor lending decisions can lead to high default rates, reduced cash flow, and financial losses.

This project analyzes historical loan data to identify portfolio performance, customer characteristics, and risk factors that influence loan defaults.

---

## Business Objectives

The analysis aims to answer the following business questions:

1. How is the overall loan portfolio performing?
2. What percentage of loans are considered Good Loans and Bad Loans?
3. Which loan purposes receive the highest funding?
4. Which customer segments have the highest default rates?
5. How do loan grades affect default risk?
6. Does Debt-to-Income (DTI) ratio influence loan performance?
7. Which states contribute the most loan applications?
8. Which borrower characteristics are associated with higher credit risk?

---

## Business Goals

The insights generated from this project can help financial institutions:

- Improve loan approval strategies
- Reduce default risk
- Identify high-risk customer segments
- Optimize lending policies
- Improve portfolio profitability
